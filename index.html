Supporting product design to connect Theory and Design 2015-present

The goal of this study is to provide recommendations for the design of informational resources to bridge the gap between Theory and Design. Academic research provides important insights for product design, however, past work shows that academic findings are rarely applied in industry. I interviewed 18 design practitioners to identify what informational resources they use in their work and how these support their workflow. I prompted participants with design challenges, producing 18 outcomes which I compared to the interviews. Now, I am designing a website to assist designer's workflow based on my study's findings. It will work as a search engine to find solutions to specific design problems. The next step is conducting in-context usability tests of the tool.

  • Grounded theory approach (coding, reporting)

Social comparison feedbacks 2014-2015

This study provides design implications for helping designers create social comparisons that motivate higher game performance. Designers often make use of social comparisons to motivate people to perform better. I created two different feedbacks based on the concept of closeness to comparison. Specifically, I tested (1) comparing users to a target described as a similarly experienced player, and (2) adjusting the visual representation of performance so player scores appear closer to the comparison target. We evaluate the effects of these strategies for social comparison on player performance in a controlled experiment with 425 participants, followed by a survey. Both feedback techniques improved game performance, but only for experienced players. Read the note.

Ethnography of a sharing economy NGO 2015

Non-profit organizations operate with limited or infrequent funding and personnel. As these organizations encounter increasing demand for their services, they face constraints that require them to creatively adapt their work practices in the short term, through forms of contingent action. In the long term, it is necessary for organizations to adapt and re-articulate their practices. Our research team conducted fieldwork in the West Seattle Tool Library (WSTL) from April to June 2015. A tool library functions much like a traditional book library, lending out tools and equipment to patrons. We asked, “How does the core team of the WSTL manage its inventory, labor and finances in the face of increasing demand?” Our findings surfaced the strategies used by a small non-profit as it responds to membership growth and increasing demand on its resources. Read the report.

Trusting the email campaign builder 2014

We identified low engagement in the email campaign builder when I worked for Resultados Digitais. A first set of potential issues were identified in-house through a participatory design session with customer relationship managers. Their insights informed the creation of an interview protocol around client's practices for email campaign creation. After talking with 6 of our customers, I identified a set of opportunities to increase Trust in the use of our tool. Supported by this qualitative data, I designed a prototype, used to inquire clients in a follow-up conversation, which informed a second iteration. The resulting prototype serves as a reference for future versions of the email feature.
